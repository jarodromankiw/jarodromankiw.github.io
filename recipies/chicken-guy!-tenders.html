Chicken Guy! Tenders

These are, quite simply, the best chicken tenders you'll ever taste! They're juicy and tender on the inside, but super crunchy on the outside. Air frying them keeps them nice and crisp without adding any extra oil. This is how we make 'em at my Chicken Guy! restaurants each and every day.

Ingredients

Brine

  • 1 lemon, cut in half
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/8 cup kosher salt
  • 1/8 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced or crushed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 cups ice water
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup pickle juice
  • 2 chicken breasts

Seasoned Flour

  • 130g all-purpose flour
  • 40g cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons granulated garlic
  • 3 tablespoons onion powder
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 2 tablespoons freshly ground black pepper

Egg Wash

  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup water

Breading

  • 120g panko breadcrumbs
  • 40g cornstarch
  • 30g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon granulated garlic
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt

Instructions

Brine the Chicken

  1. Place the lemon, water, salt, sugar, bay leaf, garlic, and pepper into a saucepan. Bring to a boil and cook for 5 minutes, stirring to dissolve salt and sugar.
  2. Strain the brine and discard solids. Add the ice water and stir until fully chilled.
  3. Whisk in buttermilk and pickle juice.
  4. Cut chicken breasts into tender-sized strips and add to the brine. Soak for up to 2 hours.

Make the Seasoned Flour

  1. Sift the flour and cornstarch into a bowl to remove lumps.
  2. Add all spices and whisk well to combine evenly.

Prepare the Breading

  1. Sift the flour and cornstarch into a bowl.
  2. Add panko and all spices. Whisk thoroughly to combine.

Make the Egg Wash

  1. Whisk eggs and water together until fully blended and smooth.

Bread the Chicken

  1. Set up 3 bowls: seasoned flour, egg wash, and breading.
  2. Coat each chicken strip in seasoned flour and shake off excess.
  3. Dip in egg wash, let excess drip off, then transfer to breading mixture.
  4. Press flat to about 1/4-inch thick. Flip and press again to ensure even coating and shape.

Cook the Chicken

  1. Lightly spray with cooking spray and place in air fryer.
  2. Cook at 350°F for 10–12 minutes, or until golden brown and fully cooked through.
  3. Serve hot with your favorite sauces.
