Fudgy Brownies

Rich, moist, and deeply chocolatey—these fudgy brownies are perfect for when you're craving a decadent treat. Easy to make and hard to resist, this is a go-to recipe for every brownie lover.

Ingredients

    Brownies

  • 145g of vegtable oil
  • 1-1/4 cups (250g) granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons (75g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 2 large cold eggs
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
  • 2/3 cup (80g) chopped walnuts, pecans, or chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

    For the Brownies

  1. Position an oven rack in the middle of the oven and heat to 325°F (162°C). Line the bottom and sides of an 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper or foil, leaving overhangs on two sides for easy removal.
  2. Add butter to a medium saucepan over medium-low heat. Cook until fully melted, then turn off heat. Do not let it brown.
  3. While butter is hot, stir in sugar, cocoa powder, vanilla, and salt. Stir until fully combined. Batter may look gritty—this is normal.
  4. Let mixture cool for 5 to 10 minutes until warm but not hot to the touch.
  5. Add eggs one at a time, stirring vigorously after each until smooth and glossy.
  6. Stir in flour and mix until batter becomes thick and pulls away from the sides of the bowl (about 40–50 strokes).
  7. Fold in nuts or chocolate chips, if using.
  8. Spread batter evenly into the prepared pan. It will be thick—press it into corners and smooth the top as best as you can.
  9. Bake until edges look dry and center appears just underbaked, about 20–30 minutes. A toothpick should come out moist but not wet.
  10. Cool completely before removing and slicing into 16 squares. For cleanest edges, chill in the fridge for 1–2 hours before cutting.
  11. Bake at 325°F for 18 minutes for fudgier brownies (adjust if needed based on your oven).
