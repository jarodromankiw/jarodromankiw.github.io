Extra Saucy Shrimp Scampi

Makes 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 270g linguine (or spaghetti, angel hair, or fettuccine)
  • 21 large shrimp, peeled & deveined (about 300g total)
  • 3 tablespoons (42g) unsalted butter, divided
  • 3 tablespoons (45ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • 30g shallot, finely diced (or onion)
  • 8g (2 large cloves) garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 120ml dry white wine (such as Sauvignon Blanc)
  • 200ml chicken stock
  • 200ml whole milk
  • 60ml fresh lemon juice (about 1 lemon)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 40g Parmesan cheese, finely grated
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta until 1 minute shy of al dente. Reserve 1 cup (240ml) pasta water, then drain and set aside.
  2. Step 2: In a large skillet over medium-high heat, melt 1 tablespoon butter with 1 tablespoon olive oil. Season shrimp with salt and pepper. Cook 1â€“2 minutes per side until pink and just cooked through. Remove to a plate.
  3. Step 3: Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 2 tablespoons butter and 2 tablespoons olive oil to skillet. Stir in shallot and cook for 2 minutes until softened. Add garlic and red pepper flakes; cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Step 4: Pour in white wine, scraping up browned bits. Simmer for 2 minutes until reduced by half. Add chicken stock and simmer 2 minutes more.
  5. Step 5: Stir in milk, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Bring to a gentle simmer (do not boil hard). Whisk in Parmesan until smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. Step 6: Add pasta to skillet and toss to coat, adding reserved pasta water a splash at a time until sauce is glossy. Return shrimp to skillet and toss gently to heat through.
  7. Step 7: Serve hot, garnished with parsley, extra Parmesan, and a lemon wedge.
